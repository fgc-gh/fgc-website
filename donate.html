Support the work.

Fertile Ground Conservancy is a small organization doing work that larger institutions won't. Your donation funds land protection, the organizational infrastructure that makes fiscal sponsorship possible, and the ongoing work of building a conservation movement rooted in biocentric values.

Recent work includes launching the Conservation Defense Initiative and taking ownership of our first held parcel in the Elk Creek watershed (15 acres, acquired 2025).

We are a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. All donations are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law. EIN: 27-1194001.

Other ways to give

  • Stock or securities — contact us at info@fertilegroundconservancy.org to arrange a transfer
  • Planned giving / bequest — include Fertile Ground Conservancy in your will or estate plan. Contact us to discuss options
  • Land donation — if you own land you would like to protect, we'd like to talk
Where it goes
  • Land holding — property taxes, legal fees, and long-term holding costs for the Elk Creek parcel and future acquisitions
  • Fiscal sponsorship infrastructure — accounting, compliance, grant management, and organizational support for sponsored initiatives
  • Operations — insurance, filing fees, communications, and the basic costs of keeping a nonprofit functional
  • Future land acquisition — building toward additional parcels in the Elk Creek watershed and elsewhere in Cascadia

We operate on the unceded territories of the Tolowa, Yurok, Karuk, and other Indigenous peoples of the Pacific Coast. These nations have lived in constitutive relationship with this land for thousands of years — not managing it, not owning it, but belonging to it in a way that shaped their language, governance, ceremony, and body of knowledge. What FGC is trying to practice, they have been practicing since long before conservation was a word. We are working to act in a manner consistent with that prior claim, and we fall short of it regularly. We name this not as ceremony but as accountability.
